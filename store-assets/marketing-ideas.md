# SIPPY — Marketing Ideas

Tailored to SIPPY specifically: a comedy push-your-luck arcade game with a built-in short-form video
hook. This is a **casual/entertainment** app — pitch it as *fun, shareable, quick*, not as a utility.

## Target audience profile
- **Who:** Casual mobile gamers 16–34 who like tense, funny, one-more-run games (think *Flappy Bird*,
  *Crossy Road*, *Helix Jump*, *Vampire Survivors*-lite reflexes). People who send each other high
  scores in the group chat. Short-form video natives.
- **What they respond to:** a satisfying near-miss, a funny fail, a number to beat, a thing to unlock.
- **Where they are:** TikTok, YouTube Shorts, Instagram Reels, r/iosgaming & r/AndroidGaming,
  Discord servers, and mobile-game Twitter/X.

## The core viral asset (do this first)
SIPPY was **designed** around one shareable loop (spec §6): the squeaky **"one more sip…" → slap →
harp** three-beat. Every marketing clip should end on either a **CLUTCH** (triumph) or a **SPLAT**
(comedy). Both are inherently loopable and rewatchable. Lead with the fail — fails travel further.

## Relevant subreddits
Most ban direct self-promo; lead with genuine value (a clip, a tip, a "made this") and only mention
the app when asked. Read each subreddit's rules first.
- **r/AndroidGaming** — has scheduled self-promo / "what are you playing" threads; use those.
- **r/iosgaming** — same audience if you ship iOS later; developer posts tolerated with a demo.
- **r/IndieGaming** & **r/indiegames** — "I made a game" posts do well with a good GIF.
- **r/playmygame** — explicitly for feedback on your game; ideal for a beta/no-ads build.
- **r/incremental_games** — partial fit (upgrade economy); only if you lean into the blood-bank meta.
- **r/gamedev** & **r/devblog** — share the *making-of* (procedural Sippy, WebAudio synth) as a story.
- **r/Unity3D / r/haxe / webdev** — the "HTML5 canvas game shipped to Play via Capacitor" angle is a
  genuinely interesting technical post; devs upvote real build stories.

## Facebook groups / forums / Discords
- Mobile & indie game Discord servers (e.g. indie game dev communities, "playtest my game" servers).
- Facebook: "Indie Game Developers", "Mobile Gamers", casual-game enthusiast groups.
- **TouchArcade** and **r/iosgaming**-adjacent forums for the mobile-arcade crowd.
- HTML5/js game portals (itch.io devlog, HTML5 game subreddits) for a free web build as a funnel.

## Content angles (lead with value, not an ad)
1. **"0.05 seconds from death"** — a supercut of frame-perfect clutch escapes. Slow-mo the last frame.
2. **"He kept the hat"** — a montage of ghosts floating up still wearing crown / propeller / crocs.
   The joke sells the cosmetics without a "buy now."
3. **"Rate my greed"** — post a big banked-mL run and ask people to beat it. Screenshot bait.
4. **Making-of short** — "this whole game is drawn in code, no art files" over the procedural Sippy.
   Dev-curious audiences love this and it's true (CLAUDE.md art rule).
5. **Duet/stitch bait** — a clip that ends right at the WINDUP with "clutch or splat?" so people quote
   it with their guess.

## Seasonal hooks
- Weak seasonality (it's evergreen), but **summer = mosquito season**: "the only mosquito you'll root
  for" campaigns land June–August. Halloween fits the ghost/splat comedy for an October push.

## Cross-promo note
Link SIPPY to other Gen Art Studios casual/entertainment titles that share the "quick, funny,
shareable" audience via an in-app cross-promo slot. Avoid cross-promoting utility/pro tools here —
different intent, different user.

## One honest caveat
Do **not** astroturf or spam subreddits/Discords with drive-by links — it gets the app and the brand
banned and torches goodwill. The strategy that works for a game like this is simple: **make one
genuinely funny 8-second clip, post it natively where the audience already is, and let the near-miss
do the work.** Ship a free, no-ads build for feedback communities so your first impression isn't an
ad.
